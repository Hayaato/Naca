Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. It is about 1/6th the size of Earth and has a surface covered with craters, plains, and mountains.

The Moon's surface is primarily composed of rock and dust, giving it a grayish appearance. Its lack of atmosphere means extreme temperature fluctuations, and its surface features remain largely unchanged over millions of years.

The Moon plays a crucial role in stabilizing Earth's tilt, which influences the planet's climate, and is responsible for ocean tides due to its gravitational pull.
